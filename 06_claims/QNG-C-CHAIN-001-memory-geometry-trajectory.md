## Statement

## Internal chain

1. native memory exists at the update-law level
2. memory leaves a coarse-grained effective-layer signature
3. the effective layer splits into coherence-sensitive and memory-load-sensitive pieces
4. the coherence-sensitive piece participates in emergent geometry or effective force structure
5. trajectory observables may inherit lag-like directional effects from the memory-load-sensitive piece

## Important restriction

This chain does not yet claim:

- that `chi = m/c`
- that one universal `tau` exists
- that any current phenomenological fit is uniquely correct

Those are stronger downstream claims and must be tested separately.

## Downstream tests

- future QNG-core test of native memory existence
- future geometry-estimator test
- future trajectory reduction test
- future CPU/GPU agreement test for the relevant numerical object

## Known limitations

The weakest links in the old project sat in the lower part of the chain:

- mapping memory variables to observed amplitudes
- mass-scaling of `chi`
- universal `tau`

So the rebuild treats the upper half of the chain as conceptually stronger than the lower half.
