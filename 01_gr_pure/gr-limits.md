# GR Limits And Regimes

Type: `note`
ID: `NOTE-GR-001`
Status: `draft`
Author: `C.D Gabriel`

## Objective

Separate the GR regimes that were often mixed together in the legacy workspace.

## Regime A: Exact GR

Use when discussing:

- full tensor equations
- exact covariance
- exact action variation
- exact geodesic structure

## Regime B: Weak-field GR

Use when:

- `g_{mu nu} = eta_{mu nu} + h_{mu nu}`
- `|h_{mu nu}| << 1`
- Newtonian and post-Newtonian approximations are appropriate

Typical objects:

- Newtonian potential
- lapse perturbation
- spatial metric perturbation
- linearized curvature

## Regime C: PPN / observational proxy layer

Use when testing or communicating:

- `gamma`
- `beta`
- Shapiro-like delays
- equivalence-principle proxies

This regime is not exact GR. It is a reduced observable layer.

## Hard rule

Future files must declare which regime they use.

No statement may be sold as exact GR if it actually lives only in the proxy layer.
