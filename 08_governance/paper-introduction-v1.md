## Introduction

Any attempt to build a theory beyond the current split between general relativity and quantum mechanics faces two simultaneous problems. The first is structural: spacetime geometry and quantum propagation are presently described in different languages, with different primitive objects and different reduction procedures. The second is methodological: speculative unification programs often mix ontology, bridge assumptions, phenomenological fitting, and numerical evidence too early, making it difficult to see which part of the construction actually carries theoretical weight.

The original development path of Quantum Node Gravity (QNG) suffered from part of this compression problem. Several useful ideas emerged, but too many roles were carried by too few objects. In particular, `Sigma` drifted between stability field, effective field, geometric generator, and phenomenological driver; `tau` drifted between native memory, effective lag, and data-facing phenomenology; and the identification `chi = m/c` was allowed to carry stronger ontological weight than the supporting structure justified. The result was not the absence of theoretical content, but a framework in which foundational structure, recovery logic, and phenomenology were not separated sharply enough.

This work presents a clean-room reconstruction of QNG designed to correct that problem. The reconstruction is organized in the order

`GR pure -> QM pure -> bridge -> QNG pure -> phenomenology`

so that each layer is built only after the previous one has been stated in its own terms. In this rebuilt program, phenomenology is treated strictly as a downstream application layer, not as a source of ontology. Likewise, bridge statements between GR-facing and QM-facing sectors are not allowed to silently redefine the native theory.

The rebuilt core currently suggests that the strongest formulation of QNG is not a one-field unification claim, but a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates whose coarse-grained reduction produces a split effective layer. This effective layer contains two channels with different roles:

- `C_eff`, carrying coherence / compatibility content
- `L_eff`, carrying memory-load content

This split is the central correction introduced by the rebuild. It replaces the earlier tendency to force one scalar object to account simultaneously for geometric order, historical loading, and downstream phenomenology. Once this split is made, the architecture becomes cleaner. A GR-facing path can be derived through

`C_eff -> geometry proxy -> Lorentzian signature proxy -> weak-field assembly -> linearized curvature proxy`

while a QM-facing path can be derived through

`(C_eff, phi) -> correlator proxy -> local generator proxy -> mode/spectrum proxy`

The resulting bridge is therefore not a direct identification of GR and QM objects, but a disciplined reduction architecture in which both sectors descend from the same native dynamics through different effective projections.

This reconstruction does not claim exact Einstein recovery, exact canonical quantization recovery, or a final closed unified equation. Those stronger claims remain open and are treated as live recovery programs rather than completed theorems. What the rebuild does provide is a provisional canonical core: native memory-sensitive dynamics, a split effective layer, a strengthened GR-facing lane, a strengthening QM-facing lane, and a first controlled bridge layer including back-reaction and source-response proxies. On top of this core, trajectory, lensing, rotation, timing, and cosmology can be rebuilt as downstream phenomenology without being allowed to define the theory itself.

The contribution of the present paper is therefore architectural before it is final. It shows that QNG can be reformulated as a cleaner, dependency-traceable program in which ontology, reduction, validation, and phenomenology are no longer merged prematurely. This matters because it turns QNG from a historically accumulated framework into a controlled recovery program: one that is already strong enough to support a provisional backbone, but still honest about the exact recovery theorems it has not yet earned.
