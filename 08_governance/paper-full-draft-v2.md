## Working title

`Quantum Node Gravity As A Memory-Sensitive Relational Update Theory With Split Effective Layer`

## Abstract

We present a clean-room reconstruction of Quantum Node Gravity (QNG) designed to separate foundational structure from phenomenological fitting. The rebuilt theory is organized in the order `GR pure -> QM pure -> bridge -> QNG pure -> phenomenology`, preventing ontology, bridge assumptions, and data-facing claims from being merged prematurely. In the rebuilt core, QNG is formulated as a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates whose coarse-grained behavior yields a split effective layer consisting of a coherence-sensitive field `C_eff` and a memory-load field `L_eff`. This split replaces the overloaded single-field structure used in earlier formulations.

From this effective layer, we construct a geometry-facing path `C_eff -> geometry proxy -> Lorentzian signature proxy -> weak-field assembly -> linearized curvature proxy`, and a QM-facing path `(C_eff, phi) -> correlator proxy -> local generator proxy -> mode/spectrum proxy`. We further define a first back-reaction proxy closure and a source-response consistency step linking the bridge source sector to the linearized-curvature sector. Downstream phenomenology is rebuilt strictly as an application layer, yielding trajectory, lensing, rotation, timing, and cosmology proxies without allowing phenomenology to define the theory itself.

The rebuilt program does not yet claim exact Einstein recovery, exact canonical quantization recovery, or a final unified field equation. Instead, it establishes a stable provisional backbone in which native memory-sensitive dynamics feed both GR-facing and QM-facing sectors through a disciplined split effective layer. This architecture substantially strengthens the internal coherence of QNG and defines a concrete next-stage program for exact recovery and controlled data confrontation.

## 1. Introduction

Any attempt to build a theory beyond the current split between general relativity and quantum mechanics faces two simultaneous problems. The first is structural: spacetime geometry and quantum propagation are presently described in different languages, with different primitive objects and different reduction procedures. The second is methodological: speculative unification programs often mix ontology, bridge assumptions, phenomenological fitting, and numerical evidence too early.

The earlier development path of QNG contained useful ideas, but too many roles were carried by too few objects. In particular, `Sigma` drifted between stability field, effective field, geometric generator, and phenomenological driver; `tau` drifted between native memory, effective lag, and phenomenological delay; and `chi = m/c` was allowed to carry stronger ontological meaning than the support justified.

This paper presents a clean-room reconstruction designed to correct that compression. The reconstruction is organized in the order `GR pure -> QM pure -> bridge -> QNG pure -> phenomenology`, and this ordering is treated as a scientific control rather than a presentation choice. The rebuilt theory is strongest not as a one-field unification claim, but as a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates whose coarse-grained behavior yields a split effective layer:

- `C_eff`
- `L_eff`

From this layer, the paper derives a GR-facing path through geometry and weak-field assembly, and a QM-facing path through correlator, generator, and spectrum structure. The resulting bridge is not a direct identification of GR and QM objects, but a disciplined reduction architecture.

## 2. Methods

The present work uses a clean-room reconstruction protocol rather than a cumulative edit of the earlier project. The older workspace is treated as historical source material, not as canonical theory structure.

Each workspace layer has a strict role:

- `01_gr_pure`: GR-only objects independent of QNG primitives
- `02_qm_pure`: QM-only objects independent of QNG primitives
- `03_gr_qm_bridge`: bridge and consistency layers
- `04_qng_pure`: native QNG ontology and update structure
- `05_phenomenology`: downstream application layers
- `06_claims`: dependency-traceable claims
- `07_validation`: preregistration, tests, and artifacts
- `08_governance`: freezes, status maps, and manuscript control

The rebuilt native theory starts from a minimal substrate-level backbone:

- a relational substrate
- local node state containers
- a local history-sensitive update law

The key reconstruction result is a split effective layer rather than a one-field compression:

- `C_eff` as coherence / compatibility content
- `L_eff` as memory-load content

Recovery is organized through ladders rather than theorem claims. The GR-facing ladder is `C_eff -> geometry proxy -> Lorentzian signature proxy -> weak-field assembly -> linearized curvature proxy`. The QM-facing ladder is `(C_eff, phi) -> correlator proxy -> local generator proxy -> mode/spectrum proxy`. The bridge ladder includes back-reaction proxy closure and source-response consistency.

The manuscript uses three support levels:

- `proxy-supported`
- `candidate`
- `open`

Validation is hardware-explicit:

- CPU is the correctness lane
- GPU is the scale lane
- CPU/GPU agreement is required where both implementations exist

Phenomenology is treated strictly as downstream consequence. The paper is written under a restraint rule: support architecture strongly, support proxy ladders honestly, and mark exact recovery programs as unfinished.

## 3. Results

The rebuilt native update law supports a stable memory-sensitive reference implementation and a split effective reduction into `C_eff` and `L_eff`. This is one of the strongest structural results of the rebuild because it resolves the older tendency to overload a single scalar with incompatible tasks.

The native update has also passed a direct CPU/GPU agreement test, with

`max_diff = 1.3322676295501878e-15`

which is well below the declared tolerance. The native update therefore behaves as a coherent numerical object across correctness and acceleration lanes.

On the GR-facing side, the strongest present support chain is

`C_eff -> geometry proxy -> Lorentzian signature proxy -> weak-field assembly -> linearized curvature proxy`

This means the rebuilt theory has moved beyond isolated geometric surrogates into a coherent weak-field assembly picture.

On the QM-facing side, the strongest current chain is

`(C_eff, phi) -> correlator proxy -> local generator proxy -> mode/spectrum proxy`

The important clarification is that the QM lane is presently generator-led and spectrum-led rather than continuity-led.

The bridge layer now includes:

- back-reaction proxy closure
- source-response consistency

In the history-enabled source-response audit, the geometry-only fit ratio is

`0.7126219623332939`

while the source-augmented fit ratio is

`0.44926739673107696`

corresponding to an improvement of

`0.26335456560221693`

This shows that the bridge is no longer only conceptual coexistence between sectors.

Downstream phenomenology is now supported across five branches:

- trajectory
- lensing
- rotation
- timing
- cosmology

These branches are derived from the rebuilt core rather than allowed to define it.

## 4. Discussion

The main success of the rebuild is not that it has completed unification, but that it has converted QNG into a technically legible reconstruction program. The central structural correction is the move from scalar overloading to the split effective layer `(C_eff, L_eff)`. Once this split is imposed, geometry can descend primarily from `C_eff`, while lag-like and memory-sensitive structures can descend primarily from `L_eff`.

One of the most useful findings of the rebuild is the asymmetry between the two recovery lanes:

- the GR-facing lane is currently stronger than the QM-facing lane

This asymmetry is informative rather than embarrassing. It suggests that the rebuilt substrate is currently closer to geometric weak-field assembly on one side and local complex update structure on the other.

The bridge also matters more than before. It is no longer just a shared vocabulary between sectors; it now contains a first source-response step in which one side improves the other.

Phenomenology is now more trustworthy precisely because it is less powerful. It is downstream of a clearer core and is no longer allowed to rewrite ontology by fit success alone.

The main unresolved zones remain:

- exact GR recovery
- exact QM recovery
- exact back-reaction closure
- final matter interpretation
- final `Sigma` status
- any robust universal `tau`
- any strong version of `chi = m/c`

The best current interpretation of rebuilt QNG is therefore a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates whose coarse-grained reduction yields a split effective layer from which geometric, quantum-facing, bridge, and phenomenological structures can be derived in a disciplined order.

## 5. Conclusion

This paper presented a clean-room reconstruction of QNG designed to separate foundational structure from phenomenology, bridge language, and validation artifacts. The main positive result is that QNG can now be formulated as a disciplined recovery architecture rather than as a compressed accumulation of partially overlapping ideas.

The main limit is equally clear. The present rebuild does not establish exact Einstein recovery, exact canonical quantization recovery, exact bridge closure, final matter ontology, or a final universal `tau`. Those remain open problems.

The appropriate conclusion is therefore not that QNG is finished, but that it has become technically legible. The rebuilt program now has a stable enough provisional center to justify a serious next-stage recovery program and a strict enough governance structure to prevent future strengthening from collapsing back into old ambiguities.
