# Paper Contribution List v1

Type: `note`
ID: `NOTE-GOV-012`
Status: `draft`
Author: `C.D Gabriel`

## Objective

State the paper contributions in a form that is precise enough for manuscript control and restrained enough to avoid overselling.

## Contribution framing rule

Each contribution must be stated as one of:

- architectural
- methodological
- proxy-supported theoretical
- open-programmatic

The paper should not present open-programmatic items as completed recoveries.

## Contribution set

### Contribution 1: clean-room reconstruction protocol

Type: `architectural`

The paper reconstructs QNG in the order

`GR pure -> QM pure -> bridge -> QNG pure -> phenomenology`

instead of letting phenomenology, bridge assumptions, and ontology grow in mixed form.

Why it matters:

- it makes dependency tracing possible
- it prevents downstream fitting from defining primitives

### Contribution 2: correction of scalar overloading

Type: `architectural`

The paper replaces the overloaded single-field pattern of the old project with a split effective layer:

- `C_eff`
- `L_eff`

Why it matters:

- coherence-sensitive and memory-load content are no longer forced into one scalar
- geometry and lag-facing sectors can be derived separately

### Contribution 3: native memory-sensitive substrate statement

Type: `methodological`

The paper relocates memory from fitted phenomenology into the native update law of the substrate.

Why it matters:

- memory becomes part of theory construction, not just part of downstream interpretation

### Contribution 4: GR-facing recovery lane

Type: `proxy-supported theoretical`

The paper presents the derived GR-facing path:

`C_eff -> geometry proxy -> Lorentzian signature proxy -> weak-field assembly -> linearized curvature proxy`

Why it matters:

- this is currently the strongest recovery lane in the rebuild
- it provides a disciplined route from native dynamics to geometric response

### Contribution 5: QM-facing recovery lane

Type: `proxy-supported theoretical`

The paper presents the derived QM-facing path:

`(C_eff, phi) -> correlator proxy -> local generator proxy -> mode/spectrum proxy`

Why it matters:

- it shows that the rebuilt theory has a real QM-facing sector
- it clarifies that the present strength lies in generator and spectrum structure, not in continuity closure

### Contribution 6: bridge strengthening

Type: `proxy-supported theoretical`

The paper presents:

- back-reaction proxy closure
- source-response consistency

Why it matters:

- the bridge is no longer only parallel sector construction
- the source side begins to feed back into the geometric response side in a controlled way

### Contribution 7: downstream phenomenology discipline

Type: `architectural`

The paper rebuilds:

- trajectory
- lensing
- rotation
- timing
- cosmology

strictly as downstream layers.

Why it matters:

- phenomenology no longer carries ontological authority
- data-facing branches can be expanded without silently rewriting the core

### Contribution 8: freeze-vs-experimental separation

Type: `methodological`

The paper explicitly separates:

- provisional core
- experimental lanes
- open recovery programs

Why it matters:

- this prevents ambiguity about what is supported, what is candidate, and what remains open

### Contribution 9: next-stage recovery program

Type: `open-programmatic`

The paper identifies exact recovery as a live program rather than a finished result:

- exact GR recovery
- exact QM recovery
- exact closure
- final matter interpretation

Why it matters:

- the manuscript remains honest while still defining a serious forward program

## Non-contributions

The paper does not currently contribute:

- exact Einstein recovery
- exact canonical quantization recovery
- final unified field equation
- final `Sigma` ontology
- final `chi` ontology
- universal `tau`

## Suggested manuscript summary sentence

The present paper contributes a disciplined reconstruction architecture, a split effective layer, strengthened GR-facing and QM-facing proxy lanes, and a controlled bridge/phenomenology stack, while leaving exact recovery theorems explicitly open.
