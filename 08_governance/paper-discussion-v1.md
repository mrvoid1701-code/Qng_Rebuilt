## Discussion

### 1. What the rebuild actually improved

The main achievement of the rebuild is not that it suddenly proved a final unified theory. The main achievement is that it converted QNG from a historically compressed framework into a layered reconstruction program. This is a substantive improvement, not a stylistic one.

Before the rebuild, several useful ideas existed, but they were too entangled. The same objects drifted between ontological, effective, geometric, and phenomenological roles. In particular, `Sigma`, `tau`, and `chi` were each asked to carry more explanatory load than the supporting derivations justified. The rebuild improves this by imposing layer discipline and by replacing scalar overloading with a split effective layer `(C_eff, L_eff)`.

This matters because a theory becomes easier to strengthen only after its roles are separated clearly enough to fail independently. The rebuild has achieved that separation.

### 2. Why the split effective layer matters

The single most important structural correction in the rebuilt program is the move from a one-field effective story to a two-channel effective layer:

- `C_eff`
- `L_eff`

This split should not be read as a cosmetic reparameterization. It is the clearest sign that the rebuild has identified a real structural issue in the older growth path. Once coherence-sensitive content and memory-load content are separated, the downstream theory becomes more readable:

- geometry is allowed to descend primarily from `C_eff`
- lag-like and memory-sensitive phenomenology is allowed to descend primarily from `L_eff`

That separation is one reason the rebuilt bridge is cleaner than the legacy one.

### 3. The meaning of the GR/QM asymmetry

One of the most useful outcomes of the rebuild is that it exposed an asymmetry that was easy to hide in the older project:

- the GR-facing lane is currently stronger than the QM-facing lane

This does not mean the QM side is absent. It means the strongest support appears in different places. On the GR side, the current strength lies in weak-field assembly and linearized curvature organization. On the QM side, the current strength lies in local generator and mode/spectrum organization rather than continuity-style balance.

This asymmetry is scientifically useful. It suggests that the rebuilt substrate may be naturally closer to geometric assembly on one side and local complex update structure on the other. Whether that asymmetry is fundamental or temporary remains open, but the rebuild has made it visible instead of hiding it behind vague unification language.

### 4. Why the bridge now matters more

In the earlier form of the project, it was too easy for the bridge to mean only that GR-like and QM-like quantities coexisted in the same workspace. The rebuilt program has gone further. It now includes:

- a back-reaction proxy closure
- a source-response consistency step

This does not solve closure exactly, but it changes the interpretation of the bridge. The bridge is no longer only a bilingual dictionary between sectors. It has become the first place where one sector measurably improves the description of another. That is an important threshold crossing for the theory.

### 5. Why phenomenology is now more trustworthy

The rebuild also changes how phenomenology should be read. Trajectory, lensing, rotation, timing, and cosmology now appear as downstream layers built after the native update, effective split, and bridge ladders. This is a major correction to the old growth path.

Phenomenology is therefore more trustworthy in one sense and less powerful in another:

- more trustworthy, because it is now downstream of a clearer core
- less powerful, because it is no longer allowed to define ontology by fit success alone

That tradeoff is a gain, not a loss. It makes later data confrontation more meaningful because it reduces the chance that successful fitting is secretly rewriting the theory.

### 6. What remains genuinely weak

The rebuild has made several weaknesses impossible to hide.

The main unresolved zones remain:

- exact GR recovery
- exact QM recovery
- exact back-reaction closure
- final matter interpretation
- final ontic status of `Sigma`
- any robust universal `tau`
- any strong version of `chi = m/c`

This means the paper should be explicit that the rebuilt program is strongest as a controlled recovery architecture, not as a final proof of unification.

### 7. Best current interpretation of QNG

At the present stage, the best interpretation of rebuilt QNG is:

- a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates
- whose coarse-grained reduction yields a split effective layer
- from which geometric, quantum-facing, bridge, and phenomenological structures can be derived in a disciplined order

That is a stronger and cleaner statement than the older project supported. It is also appropriately narrower than a claim of exact final recovery.

### 8. What the next stage should do

The next serious stage should not add more loose proxies by default. It should attack the remaining theorem-level bottlenecks:

- strengthen exact QM recovery beyond generator/spectrum support
- deepen GR recovery beyond current linearized support
- upgrade bridge closure beyond current proxy structure
- keep proxy-to-data mapping inside experimental lanes until the core is harder

This is the point of the rebuild. It has not ended the program; it has made the next stage technically legible.

## Discussion summary

The rebuilt QNG program is now stronger because it is more honest and more structured. Its best current result is not a final equation, but a disciplined architecture in which native memory-sensitive dynamics, a split effective layer, a stronger GR-facing lane, a strengthening QM-facing lane, and a nontrivial bridge can coexist without being forced into premature closure. That is enough to justify a serious next-stage recovery program.
