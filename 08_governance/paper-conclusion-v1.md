## Conclusion

This paper presented a clean-room reconstruction of Quantum Node Gravity designed to separate foundational structure from phenomenology, bridge language, and validation artifacts. The reconstruction replaced the compressed growth path of the older project with a controlled order:

`GR pure -> QM pure -> bridge -> QNG pure -> phenomenology`

Within that rebuilt order, the strongest current form of QNG is not a final unified field theory, but a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates whose coarse-grained reduction yields a split effective layer `(C_eff, L_eff)`. From this layer, the rebuilt program now supports a GR-facing lane, a QM-facing lane, a first bridge/source-response layer, and multiple downstream phenomenology branches.

The main positive result is therefore architectural and theoretical at the same time. The rebuilt program shows that QNG can be reformulated as a disciplined recovery architecture rather than as a compressed accumulation of partially overlapping ideas. This matters because it produces a provisional canonical backbone strong enough to support geometry-facing, quantum-facing, and phenomenological derivation paths without allowing those layers to silently redefine ontology.

The main limit is equally clear. The present rebuild does not establish exact Einstein recovery, exact canonical quantization recovery, exact bridge closure, final matter ontology, or any final universal `tau`. Those remain open problems. The rebuild is strongest precisely because it now states those limits explicitly.

The appropriate conclusion is therefore not that QNG is finished, but that it has become technically legible. The rebuilt theory now has a stable enough provisional center to justify a serious next-stage recovery program, and a strict enough governance structure to keep future strengthening from collapsing back into the ambiguities of the older project.

## Closing statement

The rebuilt QNG program should now be treated as a controlled theory-hardening framework: one with a stronger GR-facing lane, a strengthening QM-facing lane, a nontrivial bridge layer, and a downstream phenomenology stack, but whose final value will be decided by whether the next stage can convert current proxy-supported structure into exact recovery results.
