## Objective

Compress the rebuilt QNG backbone into a paper-ready manuscript spine without overstating what has been recovered.

## Working paper title

`Quantum Node Gravity As A Memory-Sensitive Relational Update Theory With Split Effective Layer`

## Core thesis

The rebuilt theory should presently be presented as:

- a relational substrate with local history-sensitive updates
- whose coarse-grained dynamics produce a split effective layer `(C_eff, L_eff)`
- from which geometry, GR-facing proxies, QM-facing proxies, and downstream phenomenology can be derived in a disciplined order

This is the cleanest statement currently supported by the rebuild.

## One-sentence claim

QNG is strongest, in its rebuilt form, not as a finished unified theory, but as a disciplined architecture in which native memory-sensitive dynamics feed both geometric and quantum-facing sectors through a split effective layer.

## Main paper risk

The main risk is overselling recovery.

The paper should not claim:

- exact Einstein recovery
- exact canonical quantization recovery
- final unification

unless those are separately proven.

## Best paper positioning

The best current positioning is:

- a reconstruction paper
- a clean architecture paper
- a theory-hardening paper

not yet:

- a final unification proof paper

## Closing claim

The paper should end by saying:

- the rebuilt QNG program now has a stable provisional backbone, a stronger GR lane, a strengthening QM lane, and a controlled bridge layer, which together justify a serious next-stage recovery program.
