## Canonical backbone

The QM backbone in this workspace is:

1. states live in a state space
2. observables are operators
3. evolution is generated by a Hamiltonian or equivalent generator
4. canonical structure is encoded by commutators
5. correlators and propagators are derived, not primitive

## Canonical pair

The clean reference pair is:

`[hat q, hat p] = i hbar`

or its field-theoretic analog.

This gives the baseline for:

- uncertainty products
- mode quantization
- vacuum fluctuations

## Hamiltonian layer

The Hamiltonian layer defines:

- spectrum
- normal modes
- time evolution
- zero-point contributions

Any future graph-native quantization must be readable as a representation of this layer, not a replacement of the layer itself.

## Vacuum and correlator layer

The clean vacuum-facing objects are:

- zero-point energy
- two-point function
- mode occupations
- uncertainty products

These are the downstream objects that later QNG files may discretize.

## Thermal/statistical extension

Thermal occupation and thermal correlators belong to a separate extension layer:

- still QM
- not yet QNG
- not yet graph-native

This matters because the old workspace often merged thermal analogies directly into graph constructions.

## Why this matters for the rebuild

The rebuild needs QM to stand on its own before it is placed on:

- a graph
- a bridge
- or a QNG substrate

Otherwise any success in graph numerics can be mistaken for a foundation of QM itself.
