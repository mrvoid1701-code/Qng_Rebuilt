# QNG Sigma Status v1

Type: `note`
ID: `NOTE-QNG-006`
Status: `draft`
Author: `C.D Gabriel`

## Objective

Fix the status of `Sigma` in the rebuilt theory.

## Legacy observation

In the old workspace, `Sigma` served too many roles:

- node-level bounded quantity
- stability score
- effective field
- source for emergent metric
- source for phenomenology
- partial analog of a gravitational potential

This is too much compression for one symbol.

## Rebuild decision

`Sigma` is currently treated as an effective field candidate, not as locked primitive ontology.

That means:

- it may later be promoted if needed
- but it is not primitive by default

## Why this is the correct stance

The old workspace never fully separated:

- `Sigma` as native substrate variable
- `Sigma` as coarse-grained field
- `Sigma` as geometry generator

Until those are disentangled, locking `Sigma` as primitive would be premature.

## What remains plausible

The rebuild still allows a strong future claim:

- `Sigma` may be the most natural effective coherence field of the substrate

This remains one of the best candidates inherited from the old project.

## Immediate consequence

Any future file that uses `Sigma` must specify one of:

- `Sigma_ontic`
- `Sigma_effective`
- `Sigma_phenomenological`

until one role is formally selected.
