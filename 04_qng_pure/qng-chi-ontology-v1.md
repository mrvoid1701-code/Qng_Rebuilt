## Section 1: Hard Facts (derived or numerically confirmed)

The following are results, not interpretations. They hold in the stated limits and are
falsifiable by running the referenced tests.

**F1 — Static slaving (DER-QNG-014, Section 3.3):**
In the simultaneous limit `(ε₁, ε₂, ε₃, ε₄) → 0` with `δ > 0`:
```
χ_i ≈ (δ/α) · (σ_ref − σ_i)
```
Chi is slaved to the local coherence deficit. It is not an independent degree of
freedom at leading order in the Newtonian regime.

**F2 — Numerical confirmation (QNG-CPU-029):**
`corr(σ_deficit, χ) = 0.78` and `corr(Δχ, σ_deficit) = 0.79` across 16 nodes,
40 steps, using the v3 law with `δ = 0.20`.

**F3 — Spatial profile (QNG-CPU-030, QNG-CPU-031):**
In quasi-static equilibrium with a point source, the chi profile tracks the sigma
deficit spatially with correlation `0.99` (1D) and `0.94` (3D). Chi is elevated near
the coherence-depleted source and decays toward zero in the bulk.

**F4 — Matter sector consequence (DER-QNG-013, updated):**
Under F1, `L_eff ≈ −(δ/α)·δ_C`, and `M_eff ∝ −δ_C`. The matter identification
`ρ_eff = ρ₀·M_eff/Δu³` is a derived result in the Newtonian limit, not a
declared bridge.

**F5 — Regime of validity:**
F1 through F4 are valid only in the quasi-static weak-field low-memory limit
`(ε₁, ε₂, ε₃, ε₄) → 0`. Outside this regime — dynamic fields, strong gradients,
`ε₄ ~ 1` (memory-dominated), or Planck-scale fluctuations — chi's behavior is
not yet derived and may be qualitatively different.

## Section 2: Proposed Interpretation

**This section is labeled as interpretation. It is not derived. It is a candidate
ontological assignment that must be tested before it is promoted to a claim.**

Given F1–F5, the minimal interpretation consistent with the hard facts is:

> `χ_i` is a local measure of **coherence suppression** at node `i`. When the substrate
> is at its reference configuration (`σ_i = σ_ref`), chi vanishes. When coherence is
> locally depleted, chi accumulates proportionally. It encodes how far the local substrate
> element is from its ground state of maximal coherence.

**Minimal quantum-facing mapping (candidate, not locked):**

| QNG variable | Proposed role | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| `σ_i` | coherence amplitude — related to diagonal density matrix element | σ drives C_eff which sources the gravitational potential |
| `χ_i` | coherence suppression load — related to off-diagonal decay rate | χ is generated by σ deficit and sources matter density |
| `φ_i` | phase memory — related to quantum phase | φ drives the QM-facing propagator in existing tests |

**What this mapping does NOT claim:**
- It does not claim that `(σ, χ, φ)` generates a unitary quantum state space.
- It does not claim that the v3 dynamics conserve any quantum probability.
- It does not claim that `χ` is the decoherence rate in the Lindblad sense.
- It does not claim the mapping survives outside the quasi-static regime.

These are open questions, not defaults.

**Known failure modes in the literature:**
Emergent quantum gravity programs that mapped substrate variables directly to quantum
operators without verifying unitarity have produced spectra that violate the
Born rule or produce negative probabilities. The present mapping is not asserted to
avoid these failures — it is asserted as a candidate to be tested against them.

## Section 3: Testable Prediction

**This is the only output of this document that is actionable in the next session.**

**Prediction P1 (QM-facing φ spectrum under χ tension):**

In the quasi-static background with non-zero `χ` (v3 law, `δ > 0`), the φ fluctuation
spectrum around the background equilibrium must show:

1. A **frequency shift** of the dominant mode proportional to `⟨χ⟩`:
   ```
   Δω_mode ∝ ⟨χ⟩
   ```

2. A **mode broadening** (spectral width increase) proportional to the spatial variance
   of `χ`:
   ```
   Δσ_mode ∝ Var(χ)^(1/2)
   ```

**Why this prediction:**
If χ acts as coherence suppression at the substrate level, it must influence the dynamics
of the phase variable φ — which is the candidate quantum-phase variable. The relational
channel already couples chi to sigma gradients (v2 law) and Channel D couples chi to sigma
deficit (v3 law). These couplings should propagate to φ dynamics through the shared
neighborhood mean. A non-zero ⟨χ⟩ background should shift φ's natural oscillation
frequency. A spatially varying χ should broaden the spectrum.

**What constitutes a PASS:**
The φ autocorrelation function in the χ > 0 background decays faster or oscillates
at a different frequency than in the χ = 0 (δ = 0) background. The difference must be
statistically significant and scale with `δ` (vanish when `δ → 0`).

**What constitutes a FAIL:**
The φ spectrum is identical for `δ = 0` and `δ = 0.20` within numerical precision.
This would mean chi has no dynamical effect on the quantum-phase variable and the
proposed interpretation is incorrect at the level it claims.

**If FAIL:** χ's interpretation must be revised. It remains a gravitational-sector
variable only, and the QM-facing closure must come from a different channel.

## Section 4: What Remains Open

1. Whether the v3 dynamics preserve any form of quantum unitarity — not analyzed.
2. Whether χ plays the role of "effective mass" rather than "decoherence rate" in the
   strong-field regime — not excludable from current evidence.
3. Whether the mapping (σ,χ,φ) → (amplitude, suppression, phase) survives the
   non-linear regime (`|δ_C| ~ C_ref`) — unknown.
4. Whether χ can be independently constrained by QM spectral data, giving the second
   constraint on `(δ, ρ₀)` needed to close the Newtonian limit program — open.
