## Linearized curvature proxy

Using the assembled weak-field perturbations:

- `h_tt(i)`
- `h_xx(i)`

define the discrete second-derivative probes:

`lap_t(i) = Delta h_tt(i)`

`lap_x(i) = Delta h_xx(i)`

and the first linearized curvature proxy:

`R_lin(i) = lap_t(i) - lap_x(i)`

## Why this is a reasonable first proxy

On the toy chart this is the simplest curvature-like combination that:

- uses both temporal and spatial perturbations
- distinguishes the two slots
- and remains local and weak-field readable

## Expected consistency checks

1. remain bounded in the weak-field regime
2. correlate with the geometry-sector curvature proxy
3. correlate with gradients of the acceleration channel
4. retain history imprint
