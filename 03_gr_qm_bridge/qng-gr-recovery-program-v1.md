## Scope

This file does not claim exact GR recovery.

It defines the staged recovery ladder that the rebuild must follow if such a claim is ever to be justified.

## Recovery ladder

### Stage GR-R1: linearized metric assembly

Assemble a Lorentzian weak-field metric candidate with:

- correct sign pattern
- small perturbations
- internally consistent potential and acceleration extraction

### Stage GR-R2: linearized curvature consistency

Show that the assembled weak-field metric supports stable linearized curvature diagnostics that:

- remain bounded
- respond coherently to the same geometric sector
- and do not collapse under history/no-history comparisons

### Stage GR-R3: source-side closure

Show that the metric candidate and the matter/source sector can be paired into a controlled source-response relation.

This is not yet full Einstein recovery, but it is the first place where a field equation-like statement becomes possible.

Current rebuild update:

- a bridge-only source-response step is now proxy-supported
- an upgraded two-channel source-response step is now proxy-supported
- a first effective scalar source-matching step is now proxy-supported

### Stage GR-R4: continuum and covariance program

Show how the discrete candidate objects admit:

- a continuum interpretation
- tensorial completion
- and a meaningful covariance story

### Stage GR-R5: exact recovery claim

Only after R1-R4 can the rebuild honestly ask whether:

`G_{mu nu} + Lambda g_{mu nu} = 8 pi G T_{mu nu}`

or a controlled deformation of it is truly recovered.

## Current position

The rebuild is now beyond Stage GR-R2 and has entered the first scalar part of Stage GR-R3.
