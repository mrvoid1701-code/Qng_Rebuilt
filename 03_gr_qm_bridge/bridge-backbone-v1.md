## Clean bridge statement

The bridge layer does not yet say what spacetime fundamentally is.

It only says that a valid bridge must explain how:

- a geometric sector
- a quantum sector
- and a controlled back-reaction relation

coexist without category collapse.

## Minimal bridge tasks

Any candidate bridge must eventually answer:

1. what is classical
2. what is quantum
3. what object is exchanged between the two sectors
4. what approximation order is claimed
5. what limit recovers pure GR or pure QM statements

## Semiclassical anchor

The clean semiclassical anchor is of the form:

`G_{mu nu} + Lambda g_{mu nu} ~ 8 pi G <T_{mu nu}>`

This is not yet QNG.  
It is the benchmark for any later QNG back-reaction language.

## Representation question

The bridge may later admit discrete or graph-based representations, but those are representation choices, not primitive bridge facts.

This is important because the old project often merged:

- substrate choice
- quantization choice
- and bridge choice

into the same step.

## Success criterion for the rebuild

The bridge layer is successful if QNG can later be asked:

- which part comes from GR
- which part comes from QM
- which part is a bridge assumption
- which part is uniquely QNG

and every answer is local and explicit.
