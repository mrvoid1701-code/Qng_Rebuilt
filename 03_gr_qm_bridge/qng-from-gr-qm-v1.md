## Important clarification

QNG cannot be derived from GR and QM in the same sense that one theorem is derived from another.

Why:

- GR is an effective geometric theory
- QM is a quantum kinematic/dynamic framework
- the bridge is only partial and semiclassical
- QNG aims to be a deeper substrate-level completion

So the correct relation is:

`QNG is reconstructed as the minimal native substrate theory capable of reproducing constrained pieces of GR, QM, and their bridge`

This is an inverse-construction problem, not a direct deduction.

## Reconstruction logic

The derivation program has five steps.

### Step 1: isolate irreducible GR requirements

From `GR pure`, extract what the deeper theory must eventually explain:

- effective geometry
- curvature response
- weak-field limit
- geodesic behavior
- action-based closure

### Step 2: isolate irreducible QM requirements

From `QM pure`, extract what the deeper theory must eventually explain:

- canonical structure
- spectra and propagators
- uncertainty structure
- vacuum observables
- thermal/statistical extension

### Step 3: isolate bridge requirements

From `GR-QM bridge`, extract what the deeper theory must support simultaneously:

- classical/quantum split
- controlled back-reaction
- correspondence limits
- semiclassical consistency

### Step 4: ask what extra native structure is missing

If GR, QM, and the bridge do not by themselves specify:

- a substrate
- a local state
- a native update law
- a memory structure

then a deeper theory must introduce those.

This missing layer is where QNG enters.

### Step 5: choose the minimal native completion

QNG should add as little as possible beyond what is needed to make GR, QM, and the bridge jointly intelligible.

This is the real derivation target:

- not “invent a theory that fits”
- but “introduce the smallest substrate-level completion that can generate the required effective structures”

## Candidate minimal completion

The current candidate minimal completion is:

1. dynamic relational substrate
2. local state at substrate elements
3. local history-sensitive update rule
4. coarse-grained effective field layer
5. emergent geometry from the effective layer
6. graph/discrete representation of quantum structure

This is exactly the point where QNG becomes more than GR plus QM notation.

## What this derivation forbids

It forbids these shortcuts:

1. taking a successful phenomenological lag fit and calling it ontology
2. taking a graph estimator and calling it fundamental geometry
3. inserting `Sigma`, `chi`, or `tau` as primitives before proving they are needed
4. claiming that GR and QM uniquely force one exact QNG formula today

## What counts as success

This derivation program succeeds if we can show:

- GR constraints naturally reappear as effective geometric limits
- QM constraints naturally reappear as mode/operator limits
- the bridge naturally reappears as a controlled semiclassical limit
- and QNG contributes only a minimal substrate-level completion

## Immediate consequence for the rebuild

The next theoretical step is not phenomenology.

It is:

- define the minimal native update law
- define what effective field is generated by that update law
- define how geometry emerges from that field

Only then should trajectory, lensing, or rotation be reduced from the core.
